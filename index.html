About enjoyment

A self-motivation guide to achieve impactful deeds through enjoyment

All praises belong to God the Almighty, Who enjoins us piety. This paper has been written in order to allow its author to (i) train to author writing structured and referenced papers, (ii) investigate the topic of distraction and frivolity from Islamic perspective, (iii) share the results for the benefit of Muslims and non-Muslims and (iv) motivate the author and the readers to enjoy life thanks to Islamic prescription.

Preamble

There are numerous verses in the Qur’an that commands human kind to abandon all distractions for the sake of Him, the Lord of heavens and earths. One of the verse that I find myself representative of this is when Salomon (عليه السلام) was so pre-occupied by his horses that he forgot to remember the Al mighty. As a result, a verse was revealed which is often translated as: “Bring them back to me, and fell to slashing (with his sword their) legs and necks” (38.33). This is to emphasis the importance of avoid what will distance the people from their Lord. For a long time, I thought about this verse was about repudiating and staying away from worldly things, as it could be interpreted by one of the first verse revealed: “And uncleanliness do thou shun”(74.5).[1]
